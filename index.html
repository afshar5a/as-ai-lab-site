Break-even condition
Revenue/User ≥ CPER × Requests/User

Margin is an architectural property.

The economic behavior of AI systems is determined long before scale. Token budgets, retrieval depth, caching strategy, model routing, and orchestration shape cost per effective request and long-term resilience.

What gets modeled

  • Cost structure: input/output tokens + non-token overhead (retrieval, tools, observability)
  • Scale dynamics: MAU growth, requests/user, seasonal variance
  • Efficiency: cache hit rate, routing, compression, batching
  • Risk: margin compression, tail scenarios, failure modes

Why it matters

If architecture ignores economics, growth amplifies inefficiency. This lab develops applied models to evaluate AI systems under economic constraint—before scale exposes fragility.

Practical outcome
Identify dominant cost drivers (input vs output vs overhead), quantify break-even thresholds, and surface the fastest structural levers to restore margin.

About the Founder

Afshar Sanam founded this lab to study intelligent systems as economic systems.

Her work examines how AI architectures, inference cost dynamics, and growth patterns interact — and how structural design decisions influence long-term economic viability. She develops applied models to analyze cost sensitivity, margin resilience, and constraint-aware system behavior.

In parallel, she conducts computational research on macro-level economic signals, including sentiment modeling and narrative regime detection in policy communication.

This initiative reflects a commitment to disciplined, technically rigorous exploration at the intersection of AI systems architecture, computational economics, and structural modeling.
